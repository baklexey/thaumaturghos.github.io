Aleksei “Losha” Baklanov

IPA: [ˈloʊʃə]/[ˈløːʃə]/[ˈlʲoʂə] [bɑkˈlɑnəv]/[bˠəkˈlˠanˠəfˠ]

I am a PhD student at the department of Linguistics at the Ohio State University advised by Jessica Kantarovich.

I hold a Bachelor’s Degree in Fundamental and Computational Lingusitics, and a Master’s degree in Linguistic Theory and Language Description from HSE University (Moscow, Russia).

My research is currently focused on semantic properties of nominal temporality, namely, how inherent lexical features (relationality) interact with temporal modifiers like former, future, ex-, and -to-be. Generally, I am interested in different aspects of nominal temporality and nominal tense markers in particular: semantics, morphology, typology, and areal typology.
